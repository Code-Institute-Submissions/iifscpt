Profiles Assessment Info

IIFSC Skater Development Profiling Tool is designed with the aim to quantify and track our skater's development thereby encouraging improvement through progressive recognition of individual achievements. The following strength and conditioning assessment were selected to cover three(3) critical sport specific skill components in figure skating.

    1. Agility, Balance and Coordination

A skater strong in this component will be able to maintain steadiness whilst executing coordinated changes in directional movement pattern or abrupt stop.

complete eighteen(18) clean two-footed jumps in/out of marked hexagon in fastest time possible. i.e go around the hexagon two(2) times - start/end at the same spot.

hold a good side plank position, no collapse, in longest time possible (max120s).

hold a spiral position with good height, no collapse, in longest time possible (max120s).

start by lying supine, legs extended, feet slightly flexed, arms lifted slightly; head, neck and shoulders barely off the ground. Begin by simultaneously lifting torso and legs or bending knees towards chest to balance on gluteal muscles(buttocks). Do not touch the ground with hands, legs or balance on low-back. Return to starting position and complete as many repetition as possible in 30s.

    2. Power and Strength

A skater strong in this component will have excellent acceleration and sustained speed for jumps and spins; muscular strength and power give rise to explosive jumps and improved skating quality.

essentially a broad jump with single leg; taking off and landing with the same leg. a maximum jump distance is measured for both leg. Identifies symmetry in both leg

a maximum jump height with a fully extended body position. height of jump is measured by marking tips of fingers when body is fully extended and tips of fingers at maximum height of jump.

complete as many push up as possible in 30s.

consistent rebound jumping up and down to a marked height. Complete as many clean tuck jumps as possible in 30s.

    3. Flexibility

A skater strong in this component is able to maintain muscle and joint elasticity, as well as a full and functional range of motion.
